About RTI

The Right to Information Act, 2005 empowers citizens to seek information from government offices. This section provides details on filing RTI applications, contact information, and useful links.

RTI Act overview — परिचय

The Right to Information Act empowers citizens to request information from public authorities, promoting transparency and accountability. This section provides a short, citizen-friendly summary and key definitions for quick reference.

Key points — मुख्य बिंदु

  • Objective: Ensure access to information held by public authorities.
  • Applicability: Central, state, and local bodies funded or controlled by the government.
  • Process: Submit an RTI application with clear questions; receive a response within statutory timelines.
  • Exemptions: Certain information may be exempt to protect national security, privacy, or fiduciary interests.

Sections 1–17 highlights — धाराएँ 1–17 सार

  1. Section 1: Short title, extent, and commencement.
  2. Section 2: Definitions (public authority, information, record, etc.).
  3. Section 3: Right to information of all citizens.
  4. Section 4: Proactive disclosure obligations of public authorities.
  5. Section 5: Appointment of PIOs/APIOs.
  6. Section 6: Request for obtaining information.
  7. Section 7: Disposal of RTI requests and timelines.
  8. Section 8: Exemptions from disclosure.
  9. Section 9: Grounds to reject requests causing infringement of copyright, etc.
  10. Section 10: Severability of exempt information from non-exempt.
  11. Section 11: Third party information procedure.
  12. Section 12: Central Information Commission constitution.
  13. Section 13: Terms and conditions of Central Information Commissioners.
  14. Section 14: Removal of Information Commissioners.
  15. Section 15: State Information Commission constitution.
  16. Section 16: Terms and conditions of State Information Commissioners.
  17. Section 17: Removal of State Information Commissioners.
