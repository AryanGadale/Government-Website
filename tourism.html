Tourism in Pune District

Pune offers a variety of tourist attractions ranging from historical forts to scenic hill stations. Below are the most famous places to visit.

1. Sinhagad Fort

Location: 30 km southwest of Pune City.

Description: Sinhagad (Lion's Fort) is a hill fortress located at around 1312 meters above sea level.

  • Famous for the Battle of Sinhagad (1670) fought by Tanaji Malusare.
  • Popular for trekking and local food like Pithla Bhakri and Kanda Bhaji.
  • Key spots inside: Tanaji Malusare Samadhi, Kali Temple, and Dev Taake (water tank).
  • It offers a great view of the Sahyadri mountains.

2. Shaniwar Wada

Location: Shaniwar Peth, Pune City.

Description: A historical fortification built in 1732, it was the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire.

  • The main gate is called the Delhi Darwaza.
  • It originally had seven stories, but the top floors were destroyed in a fire in 1828.
  • A "Light and Sound Show" is conducted every evening for tourists.
  • It is a symbol of Maratha culture and architecture.

3. Aga Khan Palace

Location: Nagar Road, Yerwada, Pune.

Description: Built by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III in 1892.

  • It served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi, and Mahadev Desai during the Quit India Movement.
  • It houses the Samadhis (memorials) of Kasturba Gandhi and Mahadev Desai.
  • The palace has Italian arches and spacious lawns, making it a photographic spot.
  • It is now a museum dedicated to Gandhi's life and philosophy.

4. Lonavala & Khandala

Location: 64 km from Pune (Mumbai-Pune Expressway).

Description: Twin hill stations famous for their scenic beauty, especially during the monsoon.

  • Famous tourist spots: Bhushi Dam, Tiger's Leap, and Karla Caves.
  • Known for the production of hard candy sweet called Chikki.
  • Popular for weekend getaways, camping, and trekking (Lohagad Fort is nearby).
  • Provides a cool climate year-round compared to the city.

5. Bhimashankar

Location: 110 km from Pune (Khed Taluka).

Description: A religious and natural tourist destination located in the Sahyadri hills.

  • Home to one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva in India.
  • Surrounded by a dense wildlife sanctuary reserved for the Shekru (Giant Indian Squirrel).
  • The river Bhima originates from here.
  • Best time to visit is during the monsoon and winter for nature lovers.
